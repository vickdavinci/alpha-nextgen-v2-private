## Step 9) Optimization Recommendations

### P0 — Critical
1. **Governor death‑spiral behavior (0% scale sustained)**  
   **Evidence:** Continuous `EOD_GOVERNOR_0` with DD reaching -45%.  
   **Impact:** System sidelined for extended periods, no recovery participation.  
   **Fix:** Adjust recovery thresholds or add emergency override after X days at 0%.

### P1 — High
1. **Options spread win rate extremely low (6.98%)**  
   **Evidence:** 43 spreads, net -$14,144.  
   **Impact:** Primary performance drag.  
   **Fix:** Tighten bullish spread gating and reduce size under elevated VIX.

2. **Regime not defensive enough in 2022 H1**  
   **Evidence:** ~48% of days NEUTRAL/RISK_ON.  
   **Impact:** Allowed bullish exposure into bear market.  
   **Fix:** Increase drawdown weight or tighten thresholds for CAUTIOUS/DEFENSIVE.

### P2 — Medium
1. **Slippage spikes during shutdown clean‑up**  
   **Evidence:** 2022‑01‑26 PUT slippage 17–19%.  
   **Impact:** Expected under forced liquidations; acceptable but should be monitored.  
   **Fix:** None required; add monitoring threshold reporting.

2. **No MR activity**  
   **Evidence:** 0 MR entries/exits.  
   **Impact:** Missed diversification of returns.  
   **Fix:** Review MR entry gating and VIX filter thresholds.

### P3 — Low
1. **Missing timeline logs (PRE_MARKET, TIME_GUARD, MR_FORCE_CLOSE)**  
   **Evidence:** 0 matches in logs.  
   **Impact:** Harder to audit correctness.  
   **Fix:** Add once‑per‑day logging without inflating log volume.

## Bottom Line
Performance was dominated by **options spread losses** and a **governor clamp at 0%** that prevented recovery exposure. Regime distribution remained **too neutral** for 2022 H1, enabling bullish spread entries early. The slippage >2% on PUTs occurred during **Governor shutdown liquidation**, which is expected and not a defect.
