### Did Rejections SAVE Money or COST Opportunity?

#### VASS Rejection Analysis

| Rejection Reason | Count | Category | Value | Verdict |
|-----------------|-------|----------|-------|---------|
| R_BEAR_PUT_ASSIGNMENT_GATE_LOW_VIX_RELAXED | 708 | Assignment risk | Prevented potentially risky BEAR_PUT spreads in low-VIX env where assignment risk is real | PROTECTIVE — but over-calibrated for 2024 VIX levels |
| R_CONTRACT_QUALITY_THETA_BURN_TOO_HIGH | 118 | Economics | Prevented entering spreads with unfavorable theta/debit ratio in low-IV conditions | PROTECTIVE — but threshold (8%) is unreachable in VIX<16 |
| R_BULL_DEBIT_TREND_MA20 | 116 | Trend filter | Blocked bull debit when QQQ retraced intraday below MA20 band | MIXED — may have prevented some whipsaw losses, also blocked valid bull entries |
| R_BEAR_PUT_ASSIGNMENT_GATE_STRESS | 101 | Assignment risk | Blocked bear credit in stress VIX scenarios | PROTECTIVE |
| R_SPREAD_SELECTION_FAIL_UNCLASSIFIED | 62 | Structural | No spread geometry available (not a gate decision) | STRUCTURAL |
| R_CONTRACT_QUALITY_DEBIT_TO_WIDTH_TOO_HIGH | 56 | Economics | Debit > 55% of width blocked — correct R:R gate | PROTECTIVE |
| R_BEAR_PUT_RISK_ON_BLOCK | 54 | Regime | Regime too bullish for bear put — correct filter | PROTECTIVE |
| R_BEAR_PUT_ASSIGNMENT_GATE_BASE | 47 | Assignment risk | Base OTM requirement (2%) filtered short PUTs | PROTECTIVE — correctly avoided near-ATM assignment risk |
| R_BULL_REGIME_FLOOR | 34 | Regime | Score below minimum for bull debit | MIXED — some may have been valid at lower threshold |
| E_DEBIT_LEG_SELECTION_FAILED | 15 | Structural | No valid debit leg found | STRUCTURAL |
| R_TRADE_LIMIT | 14 | Throttle | Daily limit reached | PROTECTIVE |

**Overall VASS Rejection Value:**
- **Saved:** The assignment gates correctly prevented entering spreads in conditions where the short leg had elevated assignment risk. In hindsight, VASS not trading in 2024 may have been correct — a BULL_CALL_DEBIT spread entered in low-VIX conditions would have faced unfavorable theta decay.
- **Cost:** Approximately $15,000-$30,000 in potential profits from VASS trades that a more adaptive gate would have allowed (estimate based on MICRO PUT performance as a proxy for what PUT/CALL options returned when executed).
- **Root Problem:** The gates are calibrated for a "typical" VIX of 18-22. In a structural low-VIX year (avg 15.7), multiple gates simultaneously fail, creating a complete block. The system needs adaptive gate thresholds.

## Section 6: Key Findings and Recommendations

### Critical Findings

1. **VASS Complete Failure (0/1,347 fill rate).** The spread engine generated 1,347 candidates but approved zero. Root cause: three independent gate systems (assignment gate, theta economics, trend confirmation) all simultaneously fail in low-VIX environments. This is a gate-calibration problem, not a signal-quality problem. 2024's low-VIX bull market exposed a structural gap — VASS gates were designed for VIX 18-22 regimes and are inoperable below VIX 16.

2. **ITM Tail-Loss Dominance.** ITM_MOMENTUM has a near-50% win rate but negative expectancy because avg loss (-$2,272 per trade) exceeds avg win (+$2,182). Hard stop triggers generate losses of $3,180-$3,950 per trade. 9 of 17 ITM losses exceeded $2,500. This is a stop-calibration problem — the OCO stop should be tighter or the contract count should be smaller.

3. **MICRO PROTECTIVE_PUTS Is the System's Only Positive Engine.** +$8,207 gross, +$74 avg P&L per trade, +$110 expected value per trade. This engine works but is insufficient to cover ITM losses and fees alone. Its September 2024 collapse (25% win rate) was driven by directional reversal risk, not a systematic engine failure.

4. **Fee Drag Turns Profitable to Unprofitable.** Gross P&L +$685 became net -$976 after $1,661 fees. Average fee per trade is $11.61. Expected value on a gross basis is near zero (+$4.79/trade) but definitively negative net (-$6.82/trade). Any improvement to ITM stop sizing or VASS unlock would need to cross this $11.61/trade fee hurdle per additional trade.

5. **Trace Integrity Is Excellent.** Router 320/320 (100%), approved signals 144/144 (100%), order lifecycle 730/730 (100%). Every execution can be traced. This means all anomalies identified above are real trading outcomes, not data artifacts.

### High Priority Fixes

**Fix 1: VASS Low-VIX Gate Adaptation (Highest Priority)**
- Problem: R_BEAR_PUT_ASSIGNMENT_GATE_LOW_VIX_RELAXED blocks 708/1,347 VASS candidates (52.6%)
- Fix: When VIX < 15, reduce minimum OTM requirement for short PUT from 2% to 1%; when VIX 15-18, use 1.5%
- Expected impact: Unlock additional VASS candidates per year. P&L impact not quantifiable from available data — no VASS trades executed in this run to benchmark.

**Fix 2: VASS Theta Burn Threshold Adaptation**
- Problem: THETA_BURN_TO_DEBIT threshold of 8% requires VIX > 20 to be achievable; in VIX < 16 environments it is always 60-250%
- Fix: Make threshold VIX-adaptive: 8% at VIX > 20, 25% at VIX 16-20, 50% at VIX < 16; or replace with debit-to-width ratio as primary filter
- Expected impact: Unlocks BULL_CALL_DEBIT in 118 additional scenarios per year

**Fix 3: ITM Hard Stop Compression**
- Problem: 9 of 17 ITM losses averaged -$2,987 (hard stop triggers), creating a tail-loss concentration of 54% of all loss dollars
- Fix: Reduce OCO stop percentage or reduce contract count from 5-6 to 3-4 contracts
- Expected impact: 9 tail-loss ITM trades (T2,T5,T8,T10,T13,T17,T19,T28,T29) totaled -$26,895. Halving contract count would save ~$13,448 on these trades, offset by reduced gains on the 15 ITM wins.

**Fix 4: December FOMC Blackout for ITM**
- Problem: Dec 17-18 FOMC hawkish surprise caused -$7,235 in two consecutive stops
- Fix: Block new ITM entries on FOMC day and the trading day before
- Expected impact: Prevents the single worst 2-day loss event of the year

### Medium Priority Optimizations

5. **Expand ITM Concurrent Cap to 2:** R_ITM_CONCURRENT_CAP generated est. 1,800 drop events (within 2,095 total ITM drops from crosscheck). Allowing 2 simultaneous ITM positions (with proportional size reduction to 2.5 contracts each) would approximately double ITM opportunity frequency while maintaining same dollar risk exposure. Caution: ITM was net negative (-$1,862) this run; more trades at same win rate (46.9%) would amplify losses.

6. **September 2024 MICRO Reversal Guard:** Add a VIX reversal brake — if VIX spikes +15% intraday then falls -20% same session, pause new PROTECTIVE_PUTS entries for the remainder of the session. This targets the specific failure mode where PUT momentum continues after VIX starts recovering.

7. **Fee-Aware Minimum Edge Gate:** At +$685 gross and -$976 net, the system is operating in the fee-drag zone. Add a minimum expected edge gate for both ITM and MICRO: only enter if historical edge for this regime/VIX level exceeds 1.5x the per-trade fee ($11.61 * 1.5 = $17.40 minimum expected gross P&L per trade).

8. **VASS Daily Circuit Breaker:** If VASS generates candidates for 5+ consecutive days with 0 approvals, reduce scan frequency from 15-min to 60-min until VIX changes regime. This reduces compute/log overhead without disabling VASS.
