### 3d. TAIL_RISK_CAP Structural Analysis

The VASS_TAIL_RISK_CAP fires at $1,000 loss on a position. With 15 contracts at ~$2.80-4.00 average debit = $4,200-6,000 notional, the cap triggers at 17-24% of cost basis.

The cap provides no recovery benefit — it is a harder stop that fires too tight for the natural bid-ask range of multi-day debit spreads.

**ObjectStore confirmation:** The 80 catastrophic exit rows in the order_lifecycle data likely correspond to the 13 TAIL_RISK_CAP exits (15 contracts x ~6 orders per spread close = 90 order lifecycle events). The 0 same-session re-entries confirms the system does not compound VASS losses by immediately re-entering after a cap exit.

**Proposed fix:** Index TAIL_RISK_CAP to the spread's actual debit (cap = 35% of total debit), not a flat $1,000. At 35% of debit for a $5,250 position = $1,838 cap — which aligns with the adaptive stop range and would reduce premature exits.
