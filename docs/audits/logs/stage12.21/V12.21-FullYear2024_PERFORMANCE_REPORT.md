### VASS Tail Risk Cap Analysis (Primary Loss Driver)

**25 TAIL_RISK_CAP exits** were recorded in orders.csv — the single most frequent exit code. Each cap fires when the spread's mark-to-market loss exceeds ~1.0% of portfolio equity.

The cap is set at $1,000 on a $100K portfolio. For a 15-contract BULL_CALL_DEBIT at $3.50 net debit:
- Position size: $5,250
- Cap threshold: $1,000 = 19.0% of debit
- Adaptive stop: 25-35% of debit (depending on VIX)

The adaptive stop (P10 in the cascade) is **structurally unreachable** because the tail risk cap (P4) fires first at ~19% loss. The result is an effective stop of 19% regardless of the VIX-tiered adaptive stop parameter. This cap design worked as intended in Q1-Q2 (limiting losses), but the repeated triggering (25 times over 37 spread entries) shows the system consistently entered spreads that crossed the 19% threshold before either reaching profit targets or natural exits.

### Greeks Breach (August 22)

At 13:01 ET on Aug 22, the system held both an ITM CALL (QQQ 240906C00465000 x3) and a PROTECTIVE_PUT (QQQ 240823P00477000 x7) simultaneously. Theta across both positions exceeded the CB_LEVEL_5 threshold (-1.80 vs. -0.02 limit). All OCO orders were canceled and positions closed via risk exit. This event triggered a PUT cooldown: "LossStreak=3 | Until=2024-08-23".

The Greeks breach threshold of -0.02 Theta is extremely tight — any position with >2 cent/day Theta decay per dollar would trigger it. With multiple short-DTE positions open simultaneously, this breach was structurally predictable.

### OCO Cancel Churn

The orders.csv shows extensive OCO cancel chains on ITM positions: orders 265-296 are 16 consecutive Stop Market and Limit cancel pairs for a single ITM position (QQQ 240726C00488000 on July 10). The position ultimately filled on order 297 after 11 cancel cycles. This is OCO retry exhaustion behavior documented in V12.19 issue mapping — mitigated but not fully resolved in V12.21.
