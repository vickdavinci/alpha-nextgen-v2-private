## Key Findings and Recommendations

### Critical Findings

**1. VASS entry bottleneck: R_SLOT_VASS_CONCURRENT_MAX (437 drops, 34.1%)**
The system blocked 437 new VASS candidates because 2 spreads were already open. Given 37 approvals executed from 1,321 candidates, this single gate is blocking the most signals. The fix is not obvious: relaxing the concurrent cap would increase execution but risk cluster losses (Nov 11-15 with 3 concurrent spreads already shows the risk). The better approach is to cap by direction, not total count: allow 1 BULLISH + 1 BEARISH simultaneously rather than 2 of any type.

**2. BULL_CALL_DEBIT is the worst-performing strategy by far (16.7% win rate, -$642/spread).**
18 spreads, 3 wins, 15 losses. This is not marginal underperformance — it is structural failure. The 2.2% candidate approval rate (only 18 of 826 candidates approved) means the contract quality filters are already trying to pick the best entries. The 83.3% spread-loss rate among approved entries suggests the filters are insufficient or the strategy parameters are miscalibrated for the 2024 volatility regime.

**3. ITM stop-at-open is the dominant ITM loss mechanism (-$9,831 from 3 trades).**
Three trades with immediate stop triggers produced 75% of all ITM losses. The R:R inversion is real but would be dramatically less severe without stop-at-open events. This is a mechanical fix: 30-minute hold guard or stop-limit conversion.

**4. MICRO is structurally sound; only MICRO_OTM_MOMENTUM needs removal.**
127 approved, 124 executed, +$3,643 gross. PROTECTIVE_PUTS works. The removal of MICRO_OTM_MOMENTUM (-$1,131, 12 trades) would bring MICRO to +$4,774 gross (+$43/trade on 112 trades). The system should lean more heavily on this engine.

**5. Plumbing: 739 non-financial router rejections per year is excessive noise.**
R_CLOSE_NO_LIVE_HOLDING (343) and R_EXIT_PRECLEAR_PENDING (131) together represent 474 spurious events that indicate the exit state machine does not track in-flight close operations. This inflates order_lifecycle by 18% and complicates observability. 100% trace coverage is excellent; cleanup of these spurious events would make the traces significantly more useful.

**6. Zero BEAR_PUT_DEBIT executions despite 37 candidates.**
The assignment gate (short put >= 2% OTM required) and contract quality gates together blocked all 37 BEAR_PUT_DEBIT candidates. Whether this is appropriate calibration for 2024 conditions or overly conservative is unclear without per-candidate mark-to-market data. Given the August and September QQQ selloffs, at least some BEAR_PUT_DEBIT entries might have been highly profitable if allowed.

### High Priority Fixes

**Fix 1: Disable MICRO_OTM_MOMENTUM.**
12 trades, -$94/trade, confirmed negative expectancy. No further evidence required.
Expected impact: +$1,131/year minimum.

**Fix 2: Restructure VASS Tail Risk Cap to be stop-cascade-aware.**
Cap should not fire before the designed adaptive stop. Use: cap = max($1,000, adaptive_stop_pct x spread_cost).
Expected impact: Restructures 25+ annual exits to use designed cascade rather than emergency bypass.

**Fix 3: Add ITM minimum hold guard (30 minutes) before OCO stop-market activates.**
Three stop-at-open events cost -$9,831. A 30-minute hold or stop-limit conversion prevents gap-down immediate stops.
Expected impact: +$5,000-$9,000 based on trades in trades.csv (Jan 31, Feb 7, Jul 17).

**Fix 4: Recalibrate VASS concurrent cap to direction-level (max 1 per direction).**
Replace "max 2 total" with "max 1 BULLISH + max 1 BEARISH". This reduces cluster loss risk (Nov 11-15: 3 concurrent BULL_CALL_DEBIT = -$12,495) while potentially allowing more BEAR_CALL_CREDIT entries during bullish spread periods.

### Medium Priority Optimizations

**Fix 5: Add live-position pre-check before queuing exit orders.**
Prevents R_CLOSE_NO_LIVE_HOLDING (343 spurious events). Check Portfolio.Invested before queueing VASS close.

**Fix 6: Add in-flight close lock per position.**
Prevents R_EXIT_PRECLEAR_PENDING (131 duplicate exit events). Once a close is pending, block new exit signals for that position.

**Fix 7: Evaluate BULL_CALL_DEBIT strike selection strategy.**
16.7% win rate on 18 executed spreads is definitively wrong for a bullish market year. Either strike width is too narrow (short leg too close to long, limiting upside), or DTE is too short (insufficient time for move to develop). Review: what is the average DTE at BULL_CALL_DEBIT entry? What is the average debit/width ratio for the 3 winning vs. 15 losing spreads?
