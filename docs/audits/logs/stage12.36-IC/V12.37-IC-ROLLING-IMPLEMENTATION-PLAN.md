## Why This Change

The IC engine currently treats condors as atomic 4-leg units. When any exit fires, ALL 4 legs close together. This creates a fundamental structural problem: with C/W ~30%, the R:R asymmetry is ~2.3:1 against us. Even at 65% WR, we lose money because avg loser ($1,244) dwarfs avg winner ($307).

**Rolling** fixes this by managing the tested side independently:
- Close only the tested side early (at 0.75x side credit loss)
- Open a replacement spread further OTM to collect fresh credit
- Let the untested side continue decaying profitably

### Simulation Results (2024 Actual Trades)

| Dataset | Without Rolling | With Rolling | Improvement |
|---------|:-:|:-:|:-:|
| **V12.32 (7-14 DTE, 48 condors)** | -$13,555 / 65% WR | **+$11,553 / 85% WR** | +$25,108 |
| V12.36 (14-21 DTE, 13 condors) | -$13,517 / 31% WR | -$3,946 / 46% WR | +$9,571 |

V12.32 with 7-14 DTE is the clear winner — higher trade frequency = more premium collection, and 65% of condors are already profitable without rolling.

### Sensitivity Analysis (V12.32)

| Trigger | Recovery | Rolled | Orig P&L | With Rolling | WR |
|---------|----------|--------|----------|--------------|-----|
| 0.75x | 50% | 20/48 | -$13,555 | +$13,586 | 47/48 |
| 0.75x | 65% | 20/48 | -$13,555 | +$14,447 | 47/48 |
| 1.00x | 65% | 17/48 | -$13,555 | +$11,553 | 41/48 |
| 1.25x | 50% | 15/48 | -$13,555 | +$8,689 | 37/48 |

Result is robust across all parameter combinations. Even worst-case (1.25x trigger, 50% recovery) goes solidly positive.

### Caveats on Simulation

- Roll credits estimated at 65% of original credit (real chains may differ)
- Early close assumes trigger-point exit (real fills may have slippage)
- December crash (QQQ -5% in 2 days post-Fed) may not roll as smoothly as modeled
- Rolled spread outcomes modeled by severity heuristic, not actual chain data

## Architecture Overview

Rolling is inserted into the existing exit cascade as a new check (P2C) between MFE lock and stop loss. When triggered, it closes only the tested side and initiates a two-beat state machine:

```
Beat 1 (exit cycle): Detect roll trigger → emit side-close signals → set is_rolling=True
Beat 2 (next cycle): Detect closed side flat → search replacement → emit entry signal
```

The condor stays alive throughout — the untested side continues running. If no replacement is found, the condor may continue as a single-spread position, but that behavior is a deliberate v1 policy choice rather than a free fallback. It must be explicitly handled in state, accounting, retry logic, telemetry, and reports.

## Mandatory Design Gaps — Locked-Down v1 Policy Decisions

These gaps were identified before coding began. Each has a specific v1 policy decision that governs the implementation. **Do not deviate from these decisions without updating this document first.**

### Gap A: Partial-condor state — Three-State Model (LOCKED)

The live engine assumes one atomic 4-leg condor. A roll introduces a valid half-changed state. The v1 solution is a **three-state model**:

| State | Meaning | Set When | Cleared When |
|-------|---------|----------|--------------|
| `is_closing=True` | Broken/normal full close in progress | Full exit triggered | All 4 legs flat |
| `is_rolling=True` | Intentional roll — tested side closing, untested side live | Roll trigger fires | Replacement filled OR roll abandoned |
| Neither | Normal active condor | Construction / roll complete | — |

**Rules:**
- `is_closing` and `is_rolling` are **mutually exclusive** — setting one clears the other
- All stale-condor / half-closed reconciliation paths in `main_options_mixin.py` must check `condor.is_rolling` and **skip cleanup** for rolling condors
- The exit cycle skips both `is_closing` and `is_rolling` condors
- If a roll is abandoned (no replacement found or stale timeout), `is_rolling` is cleared and `is_closing` is set to force immediate close of the remaining side (see Gap C)

### Gap B: Campaign Accounting — Fills-Only Realized P&L (LOCKED)

Campaign accounting tracks the full economic lifecycle across original + rolled spreads:

- `cumulative_realized_pnl`: Updated **only from actual fill prices**, never from mark-to-market snapshots
- `cumulative_credit`: Sum of original `net_credit` + all roll replacement credits (per-spread basis)
- The exit cascade uses campaign-level economics when `condor.roll_count > 0`:
  - `credit_100 = condor.cumulative_credit * 100 * condor.num_spreads`
  - `combined_pnl = portfolio_unrealized + condor.cumulative_realized_pnl`
- Trade-result recording at campaign end: final P&L = `cumulative_realized_pnl` + closing leg realized

**Source of truth:** Fill handler in mixin computes realized P&L from `(fill_price - entry_price) * quantity * direction * 100` and writes to `cumulative_realized_pnl`. Trigger-time marks are never persisted as realized.

### Gap C: Replacement Policy — Same-Expiry, Close-on-Failure (LOCKED)

Replacement search constraints for v1:

| Policy | v1 Decision |
|--------|-------------|
| Expiry scope | **Same expiry only** — no next-weekly fallback |
| Entry-quality gates | **Skip** EM / symmetry / daily-limit checks for replacement. Apply only: liquidity (`_validate_leg_liquidity`), minimum credit recovery, further-OTM constraint |
| Loss-breaker | **Does not apply** — replacement is part of existing campaign, not a new trade |
| Strike reuse | **Applies** — new short strike must differ from the original short strike on the rolled side |
| Poor credit | If credit < `IC_ROLL_MIN_CREDIT_RECOVERY_PCT` (50%), **reject** the replacement — do not accept low-credit rolls |
| No replacement found | **Close remaining side immediately** — set `is_closing=True`, emit close signals. No single-spread campaign state in v1 |
| Stale timeout | If `> IC_ROLL_PENDING_STALE_MINUTES` (10 min) and replacement not filled, **abandon roll and close remaining side** |

**Rationale for close-on-failure:** Allowing single-spread campaigns requires campaign P&L accounting, exit cascade, telemetry, and reporting to all handle a 2-leg condor state. This complexity is not justified in v1 when the simulation shows rolling is profitable even at 50% credit recovery.

### Gap D: Per-Side P&L — Best-Effort Trigger Signal (LOCKED)

- `_get_side_pnl()` uses `Portfolio[sym].UnrealizedProfit` for the 2 legs of each side
- This is a **best-effort trigger signal only** — it may include pending close orders, partial fills, or stale marks
- The roll trigger fires based on this signal, but **campaign realized P&L is never updated from these marks**
- Campaign P&L is updated exclusively from fill events (Gap B)
- The per-side P&L helper must be documented with a comment: `# Best-effort trigger signal — do not use for accounting`

### Gap E: Fill Integration — Walk Before Merge (LOCKED)

Before merging Phase 4 or Phase 5, the developer must walk the following paths end-to-end and verify rolling does not break them:

1. **IC exit emission** — `_build_side_close()` emits only 2-leg close, not 4-leg
2. **Retry-close escalation** — existing retry/ladder logic must not treat a rolled-side close as a stale half-close
3. **Stale-condor cleanup** — must check `is_rolling` before treating a partial condor as stale
4. **Roll-close fill handling** — fill handler detects `is_roll_close` metadata, updates `cumulative_realized_pnl`
5. **Roll-entry fill handling** — fill handler detects `is_roll_entry` metadata, calls `register_roll_fill()`
6. **Persistence restore** — `is_rolling`, `rolling_side`, `roll_pending_since` must survive ObjectStore round-trip

**Verification method:** Add explicit unit tests for each path in Phase 7.

### Gap F: Reporting — One Campaign = One Trade (LOCKED)

- A rolled condor is reported as **one campaign/trade** in performance evaluation
- The campaign entry time is the original condor entry time
- The campaign exit time is the final close time (after all rolls complete)
- Campaign P&L = `cumulative_realized_pnl` at campaign end
- `roll_count` and `roll_history` are available for drill-down analysis but do not split the trade

**Telemetry events (new):**
| Event | Log Tag | When |
|-------|---------|------|
| Roll trigger | `IC_ROLL_TRIGGER` | Side loss exceeds threshold |
| Roll side close fill | `IC_ROLL_CLOSE_FILL` | Tested side legs filled flat |
| Replacement found | `IC_ROLL_REPLACEMENT` | Replacement spread selected |
| Replacement rejected | `IC_ROLL_NO_REPLACEMENT` | No valid replacement in chain |
| Replacement fill | `IC_ROLL_ENTRY_FILL` | Replacement legs filled |
| Roll abandoned | `IC_ROLL_ABANDONED` | Stale timeout or no replacement → close remaining |
| Campaign exit | `IC_EXIT` (existing) | Final campaign close, includes roll metadata |

## Phase 4: Roll Follow-Up + Replacement Search (`iron_condor_engine.py`)

### 4a. New method: `run_roll_follow_up()`

Called from the mixin after exit cycle. Detects `is_rolling` condors where closed side is flat, then searches for replacement. **Per Gap C: if no replacement found, close remaining side immediately (no single-spread campaigns in v1).**

```python
def run_roll_follow_up(
    self, *, condor: IronCondorPosition,
    side_is_flat_func: Callable[[IronCondorPosition, str], bool],
    chain, qqq_price: float, vix_current: float, current_time: datetime,
    effective_portfolio_value: float,
) -> Optional[List[TargetWeight]]:
```

Logic:
1. If not `condor.is_rolling`, return None
2. Stale timeout: if `> IC_ROLL_PENDING_STALE_MINUTES`, abandon roll → close remaining side (Gap C)
3. If closed side is flat: record realized P&L, search replacement
4. Call `_search_and_build_replacement()` — if found, emit entry signal
5. If not found: clear `is_rolling`, set `is_closing=True`, emit close signals for remaining side (Gap C: close-on-failure).

### 4b. New method: `_search_and_build_replacement()`

Reuses existing infrastructure:
- `_extract_chain_contracts()` — already exists
- `_find_wing_leg()` — already exists
- `_validate_leg_liquidity()` — already exists

Constraints:
- Short strike further OTM than original by `IC_ROLL_FURTHER_OTM_MIN_PCT`
- New credit >= `IC_ROLL_MIN_CREDIT_RECOVERY_PCT * original_side_credit`
- Expiry: same first (if `IC_ROLL_SAME_EXPIRY_PREFERRED`), then next weekly

Returns `(new_short, new_long, new_credit)` or None.

### 4c. New method: `_build_roll_entry_signal()`

Single-side PUT_CREDIT or CALL_CREDIT TargetWeight with metadata:
- `"is_roll_entry": True`
- `"condor_id": condor.condor_id`
- `"roll_number": condor.roll_count + 1`

### 4d. Roll fill registration: `register_roll_fill()`

- Update `condor.short_put/long_put` (or call) with replacement legs
- Set `put_side_active = True` (or call)
- Increment `roll_count`, add `new_credit` to `cumulative_credit`
- Record `RollRecord` in `roll_history`
- Clear `is_rolling`, `rolling_side`, `roll_pending_since`

## Updated IC Exit Cascade (V12.37)

```
PRE-GUARD (always fire, bypass hold guard):
  P0:  VIX Spike          — VIX >= 33
  P1:  Regime Break        — regime outside [min-buffer, max+buffer]
  P1B: Underlying Invalid  — QQQ moved >= max(config%, entry_EM) from entry
  P2:  Assignment Risk     — DTE <= 3 and short strike ITM

HOLD GUARD (DTE-adaptive hold window):
  Hard Stop               — loss >= 1.25x credit
  EOD Gate                — 15:45+, held >= 4h, loss >= 0.75x credit
  Profitable Bypass       — P&L > 0 → fall through
  Default Block           — suppress P2-P7 during hold

POST-GUARD (full cascade):
```
