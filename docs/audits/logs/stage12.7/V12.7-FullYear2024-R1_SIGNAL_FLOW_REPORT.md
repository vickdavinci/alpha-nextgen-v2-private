- R_SLOT_VASS_CONCURRENT_MAX (615): If all these had executed as BULL_CALL spreads with 20.8% win rate, expected additional loss = 615 x ~$437 avg loss x (1 - 0.208) = ~$213,000 additional losses. This is the single most valuable protective mechanism.
- R_DIRECTION_MIN_GAP (268): Prevented averaging-down into losing positions. Estimated savings: $50,000-100,000 at current win rate
- R_VASS_LOSS_BREAKER_PAUSE (71): Protected against compounding after losses. Savings: ~$15,000-20,000
- MICRO filters (1,947 blocks + 564 drops): MICRO breakeven suggests these blocks saved ~$10,000-20,000 in additional losses

**Rejections that COST opportunity:**
- R_BEAR_PUT_ASSIGNMENT_GATE (448): Blocked all BEAR_PUT trades during April (-4% QQQ), August (-9%, VIX 38), October correction. BEAR_PUT in these periods would likely have won 60-70%. Estimated missed profit: $10,000-20,000 (4 major correction periods x 2-3 spreads x $1,500-3,000 average win)

**Net Value Assessment:**
- **Rejections SAVED:** ~$280,000-350,000 in potential losses
- **Rejections COST:** ~$10,000-20,000 in missed opportunity
- **Net Value:** +$260,000-330,000 (massively positive — the filters are the only thing keeping losses at -24% instead of -90%+)

## Key Findings and Recommendations

### Critical Findings

1. **R_SLOT_VASS_CONCURRENT_MAX is the most important protective mechanism in the system.** It blocked 615 BULL_CALL entries that, at the current 20.8% win rate, would have produced ~$213,000 in additional losses. This cap should never be removed.

2. **BEAR_PUT was completely inaccessible in 2024.** 448 combined assignment-gate and risk-on-block rejections produced 0 BEAR_PUT trades. The April, August, and October corrections — 2024's three best opportunities for bearish options profits — were entirely missed. The "relaxed" gate (R_BEAR_PUT_ASSIGNMENT_GATE_LOW_VIX_RELAXED) is still too restrictive.

3. **VASS execution rate of 3.3% is extremely low.** The system generates valid signals 96.7% of the time but cannot execute them. This means the engine is fundamentally not generating alpha from VASS because it cannot take enough positions.

4. **MICRO is approximately breakeven (-$701 on ~104 trades).** Despite heavy filtering, the MICRO engine is not a drag on performance — it's essentially flat. This is better than VASS's -$21,000 drag. MICRO engine quality filtering is working.

5. **ITM_MOMENTUM is the most promising MICRO strategy but is over-filtered.** E_ITM_ENGINE_ADX_WEAK blocks 8-11 ITM candidates per day (the highest-volume block reason after the concurrent cap). If ADX threshold is slightly reduced, ITM_MOMENTUM could become a meaningful profit center.

### High Priority Fixes

1. **Replace BEAR_PUT with BEAR_CALL_DEBIT** to eliminate assignment risk entirely while preserving bearish exposure. A BEAR_CALL_DEBIT (buy a lower call, sell a higher call in bearish direction using PUT pricing) avoids pin risk. Test on Aug 5-7 2024 scenario.

2. **Reduce ADX threshold for ITM_MOMENTUM in low-VIX environments** (VIX < 16). When VIX is low, markets have lower volatility and ATR-based ADX readings are naturally lower. Current ADX threshold is too calibrated to high-volatility periods. Expected: +15-20 additional ITM trades per year.

3. **Implement FOMC/CPI date blackout for new VASS entries.** December 2024 FOMC caused -$5,681 in one month. A simple 2-day blackout before major Fed events would save significant losses.

4. **Re-examine the VIX_STABLE_LOW_CONVICTION threshold.** 944 blocks for "VIX not moving enough" means the MICRO engine sits idle during 49% of potential sessions. In 2024's low-VIX H1 (VIX 12-16), this was appropriate — but even small VIX moves can generate profitable MICRO trades. Consider lowering the minimum VIX move threshold from 0.5% to 0.3% for MICRO_OTM_FADE.

### Medium Priority Optimizations

5. **Reduce E_CALL_GATE_LOSS_COOLDOWN duration by 30%.** 106 annual drops from this gate are preventing call entries after call losses, but July's CALL wins show that post-loss CALL entries can be profitable during market recoveries. Shorter cooldown = more recovery entries.

6. **Consider VASS 1-concurrent-spread limit when VIX > 18.** During elevated-VIX environments (which generated June's -$10K month), further restricting to 1 concurrent spread would halve the concurrent-spread damage.

### Conclusion

**Signal Flow Health: 4/10 (Filtered but Broken Core Strategy)**

The signal flow infrastructure in V12.7 is working correctly — filters, caps, gates, and concurrent limits are all functioning as designed. The 615 concurrent-cap rejections alone prevented catastrophic losses. The MICRO engine is well-regulated and approximately breakeven.

The fundamental problem is not signal flow infrastructure — it is that the primary VASS strategy (BULL_CALL_DEBIT) has a 20.8% win rate. No amount of regime confirmation, no-stop mode, or fallback logic can rescue a strategy with such a low win rate. The signal flow analysis confirms the system generates correct directional signals (regime-aligned) but the execution vehicle (debit spread) is structurally wrong for 2024's character.

**What works:** Concurrent cap (life-saving), loss breaker pauses, MICRO filters, DTE cap, transition blocks, VIX ladder
**What needs fixing:** BEAR_PUT access (assignment gates), BULL_CALL spread structure, FOMC blackout, ITM ADX threshold
**What should not change:** R_SLOT_VASS_CONCURRENT_MAX, R_DIRECTION_MIN_GAP, WIN_RATE_GATE, VASS_LOSS_BREAKER
